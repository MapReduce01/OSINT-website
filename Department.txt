Simon Fraser University (SFU) is a comprehensive university located in British Columbia, Canada. It offers a wide range of academic programs through various departments across its faculties. Here are the main faculties and some of their departments, along with a brief description of each:

### 1. Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences (FASS)
- **Anthropology**: Study of human societies, cultures, and their development.
- **Archaeology**: Exploration and analysis of past human behavior through material remains.
- **Economics**: Focuses on the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services.
- **History**: Examination of past events, societies, and cultures.
- **Philosophy**: Investigates fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, and mind.
- **Political Science**: Study of politics, government systems, and political behavior.
- **Psychology**: Research and study of the mind and behavior.
- **Sociology and Anthropology**: Study of social behavior, institutions, and cultural practices.

### 2. Faculty of Business Administration (SBBA)
- **Management**: Covers areas such as organizational behavior, human resources, and strategy.
- **Finance**: Focuses on financial management, investment, and capital markets.
- **Marketing**: Studies consumer behavior, advertising, and market research.
- **International Business**: Examines business operations across national borders.
- **Entrepreneurship**: Focuses on innovation, startups, and new venture creation.

### 3. Faculty of Communication, Art and Technology (FCAT)
- **Communication**: Explores media, culture, and communication technologies.
- **Interactive Arts and Technology**: Combines art and technology for multimedia production.
- **Film Studies**: Focuses on the history, theory, and criticism of film.

### 4. Faculty of Education
- **Curriculum and Instruction**: Develops teaching strategies and educational resources.
- **Educational Psychology**: Studies psychological principles in education processes.
- **Adult Learning and Educator Development**: Focuses on education for adult learners.

### 5. Faculty of Science
- **Biological Sciences**: Covers ecology, genetics, microbiology, and organismal biology.
- **Chemistry**: Studies the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter.
- **Mathematics**: Focuses on pure mathematics, applied mathematics, and statistics.
- **Physics**: Explores the fundamental principles governing the universe.
- **Earth Sciences**: Studies the planet's structure, properties, processes, and history.

### 6. Faculty of Applied Sciences
- **Computer Science**: Covers computing theory, software engineering, and data science.
- **Engineering Science**: Focuses on engineering principles in various fields including biomedical, mechatronic, and environmental engineering.
- **Information Systems**: Studies the design and implementation of information systems.

### 7. School of Public Policy
- **Policy Analysis**: Focuses on formulating and evaluating public policies.
- **Public Administration**: Studies the management and implementation of public policies and services.

### 8. School for the Contemporary Arts
- **Dance**: Focuses on dance performance, choreography, and theory.
- **Visual Arts**: Explore practices in visual arts, including painting, sculpture, and new media.
- **Theatre**: Involves performance, playwriting, and theatre production.

### 9. School of Interdisciplinary Studies
- **Sustainable Community Development**: Focus on sustainable practices and community engagement.
  
### 10. Graduate Programs
SFU also offers a variety of graduate programs across its faculties, allowing for advanced study and research opportunities in disciplines like Business, Education, Environmental Science, and more.
