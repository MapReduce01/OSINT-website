Simon Fraser University (SFU) is a public research university located in British Columbia, Canada. Founded in 1965, it is named after the Scottish explorer Simon Fraser, who played a key role in the province's history. SFU is known for its commitment to research, a diverse range of academic programs, and its focus on community engagement and applied learning.

### Key Features of Simon Fraser University:

- **Campuses**: SFU has three campuses located in British Columbia:
  1. **Burnaby Campus**: The main campus, situated on Burnaby Mountain, features stunning views and contains most of the university's faculties and academic programs.
  2. **Vancouver Campus**: Located in the heart of downtown Vancouver, this campus focuses on programs in business, arts, and environmentally sustainable development.
  3. **Surrey Campus**: This campus offers programs in health science, business, and engineering, emphasizing innovation and community engagement.

- **Academics**: SFU offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across various disciplines, including arts, sciences, business, engineering, health sciences, education, and more. The university is known for its interdisciplinary approach and is home to several research institutes and centers.

- **Community Engagement**: SFU has a strong emphasis on integrating education with community service and outreach. It encourages students to engage with local communities through experiential learning, co-op programs, and volunteering.

- **Campus Life**: The university has a vibrant student life, with numerous clubs, organizations, and recreational activities available. SFU offers sports teams, cultural and artistic events, and various student services to support overall student well-being.

- **Internationalization**: SFU has a diverse student body, attracting students from around the world. The university promotes international learning opportunities and has numerous partnerships with institutions globally.

- **Sustainability**: SFU is recognized for its commitment to sustainability and environmental stewardship, integrating these values into its curriculum, research, and campus practices.
